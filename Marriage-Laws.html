One of the driving causes of child marriage in America are loopholes in state law that allow minors to wed. Let's look at each state's marriage laws:
 
US States Minimum age to Marry with Parental consent    Exceptions
Alabama Male: 16; Female: 16 N/A  
Alaska Male: 16; Female: 16 Minors as young as 14 may marry with parental and judicial consent.
Arizona Male: 16; Female: 16
16 year olds may marry with both parental and judicial consent, and if the two marital parties have
an age difference of no more than three years. 		N/A
Arkansas Male: 17; Female: 17 Children under 16 years may marry with both proof of pregnancy and parental consent.
California Male: 18; Female: 18 Any person under the age of 18 may marry with the consent of at least one parent.
Colorado Male: 16; Female: 16
16 and 17 year olds may marry with both parental and judicial consent. 		N/A

Georgia Male: 17; Female: 17;
17 year olds may marry if emancipated and the age difference between the two marital parties is no more than four years. 		N/A
Hawaii Male: 16; Female: 16; 15 year olds may marry with parental and judicial consent.
Idaho Male: 16; Female: 16; Minors under the age of 16 are allowed to marry with judicial approval. Judges are required to consider an expert opinion on whether or not the minor is physically and mentally able to commit.
